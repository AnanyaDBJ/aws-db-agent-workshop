Creating a balance between equities and bonds is central to designing a resilient investment portfolio. Each asset class behaves differently, and balancing the two helps manage risk while targeting growth.

Equities, or stocks, are ownership shares in companies. They offer the potential for high returns but come with higher volatility. Bonds, on the other hand, are debt instruments that provide regular income and typically exhibit lower volatility than stocks.

Younger investors, who have a longer investment horizon, can afford a higher allocation to equities since they have time to ride out market downturns. Conversely, individuals closer to retirement often shift towards bonds to preserve capital and secure stable income streams.

A commonly referenced rule of thumb is to subtract your age from 100 to determine your equity allocation. For instance, a 30-year-old might hold 70% in equities and 30% in bonds, adjusting the mix over time.

Diversifying within each asset class is important too. For equities, a mix of large-cap, mid-cap, and international stocks can spread risk. For bonds, combining government, municipal, and corporate bonds balances yield and credit risk.

Rebalancing is vital. Over time, market movements can shift the original asset allocation. Periodically reviewing the portfolio and adjusting it back to the intended mix maintains the desired risk profile.
